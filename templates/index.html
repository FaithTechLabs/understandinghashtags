#metoo

What is it?

#MeToo is a social media hashtag that seeks to raise awareness about sexual harassment and assault by asking anyone who has experienced this kind of abuse to identify themselves by saying #MeToo.



#Metoo - The People Involved

Women and men, whether victims, perpetrators or bystanders, all have voices in this conversation.

Women

  • Main group speaking about the issue
  • Main group sharing stories of abuse
  • Much less likely to be surprised by the volume of people saying “Me Too”
#MeToo: Social media flooded with personal stories of assault

Men

  • More likely to be surprised by the number of people affected
  • Fewer share stories of abuse, but some do
What 'Me Too' Can Teach Men Who Are Willing to Listen

Victims

  • Some victims feel they have a new voice
  • Some do not feel comfortable identifying themselves
Women identify as assault, harassment victims using #metoo hashtag

Perpetrators

  • Essentially silent, some opposition
  • Some called out by name by those they have abused
  • Some are recognising what they’ve done and are repentant
  • Cultural commentary on how society views their role in this issue
#MeToo named the victims. Now, let's list the perpetrators

Bystanders

  • People who were not aware of this issue (both male and female)
  • People who do not identify as a victim
  • Most likely group to voice opposition


#MeToo - The Response

Identification

  • Both men and women are choosing to say #MeToo
  • Find community and solidarity
  • Some feel it is backwards to list the victims, while the perpetrators remain anonymous
  • Should the onus be on survivors to “out” themselves as victims?
Women tweet #MeToo to share experiences of sexual harassment, assault

Silence

  • Some people choose silence on the issue
  • Does saying nothing make others assume that a person has not experienced this kind of abuse? Or agreement with it?
Not everyone with a #MeToo is posting their story. Here’s why some are refraining.
#MeToo made the scale of sexual abuse go viral. But is it asking too much of survivors?

Support

  • Men especially are choosing to show their support through other hashtags like #IWill and #HowWillIChange
  • The penultimate goal of the #MeToo movement is to have lasting change, so that less people will say #MeToo in the future. Part of the conversation questions whether that is an achievable goal
In Response To #MeToo, Men Are Tweeting #HowIWillChange

Opposition

  • Some deny the relevance and truth of this issue
  • Victim blaming/shaming
